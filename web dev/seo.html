    2. This is arguably the most important tag that you need to be familiar with when it comes to increasing your rankings.SEE TITLE

  2. Meta description tag

    3. Right after the title tag, the meta tag is an absolute must for anyone who is serious about their SEO.Think of the meta description as the synopsis on the back of a book. The title is the heading, and the meta description is the extra couple sentences of information that further describe what the content is about.If you do this right, your meta description will not only help you rank well on Google, but it will also sell the “click” with compelling copy.SEE META.discription

  3. Header tags

    4. The header tags don’t just help organize your content for readers. It also helps your SEO.When you tag all of your topical headings throughout the post, you tell search engines exactly what your content is discussing.This is how Google creates the snippets you’ll sometimes see when looking for a topic.THE HEADING THAT YOU JUST SAW

  4. Links and anchor text tags

    5. Throughout your content, you’re probably going to link to different websites.Most people will just add the link and forget about the anchor text, which is unfortunate for SEO endeavors.Your anchor text should answer these questions for Google.If you’re linking to someone else’s website and you provide a good anchor tag, it will help their rankings more than the link alone. And if you’re linking to a page on your own website, it will help your own domain authority because it keeps your content HERE YOU GO

  5. Image alt tags

    6. Occasionally, people will also search under the “Images” tab.And when they do that, the SEO game is a little bit different.Search engines can’t read images, but search engines can read words.This means that, to have a chance for your images to rank on Google, you need to include alt tags on all of your screenshots and photos.That way, when someone is searching the “Images” tab, they can still find your content.IMAGE ON YOUR RIGHT PLEASE
